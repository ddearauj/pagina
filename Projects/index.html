About me

Passionate about state of the art technology and understanding how it relates and shapes the market and our daily lives. I am an Industrial Engineering Undergraduate Student in the Universidade Estadual de Campinas (UNICAMP), Brazil. Between Fall/14 and Fall/15 I was awarded with a full scholarship exchenge program by the Brazilian Government to study at Purdue University and during the summer I was hired by Shell Internation Exploration and Production Inc. as a Subsea Hardware Engineer Summer Intern.

University really peeked my interest in software development and algorithms.During my sophomore and junior years, I developed an year long Research with UNICAMP's Electrical and Computer Engineering School on Blind Signal Separation using bio-inspired computaion.

I've always been interested and involved with volunteer work. At UNICAMP this was translated by founding along some friends the first Chapter of the Engineers Without Borders - Brazil in the state of São Paulo in September of 2013, where I worked for a year as Strategic Management and People Advisor. I also taught (lectured é muito presunçoso?) World History in the community prep school of the School of Applied Sciences aimed to reach high school students who could not afford a prep school
